Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include:

Thomas Jefferson
As the primary author of the Declaration, Jefferson articulated the colonies’ reasons for breaking away from Britain. He later became the third U.S. President and founded the University of Virginia.
John Adams
A fierce advocate for independence, Adams was instrumental in persuading Congress to declare it. He later served as the first Vice President and second President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
One of the most accomplished men of his era, Franklin was a printer, inventor, diplomat, and philosopher. He helped secure crucial French support during the Revolutionary War.
John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress at the time, Hancock is best remembered for his bold, oversized signature on the Declaration. His name became synonymous with the word “signature” in American culture.
Samuel Adams
A cousin of John Adams, he was a master of political agitation and helped organize the Boston Tea Party. He played a major role in rallying public support for independence.
Roger Sherman
A respected lawyer and politician from Connecticut, Sherman helped draft the Declaration and later the Constitution. His ability to build consensus was vital in shaping the new nation.
Robert Morris
Known as the “Financier of the Revolution,” Morris used his personal wealth and banking skills to support the Continental Army. He also helped establish the U.S. financial system after the war.

Each of these individuals played a major role not just in signing the Declaration but in shaping the early United States.
