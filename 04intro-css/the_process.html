Prelude: What is Rickrolling?

Rickrolling is the art of tricking people to click on links to the song "Never Gonna Give You Up" by Rick Astley. Many versions of rick rolls have evolved over the years and here are the key parts of a rickroll.

  • The Bait:

    Start with an irresistible hook: a misleading link that promises something exciting—like "Early Access to GTA 6!" or "You Won’t Believe This Hack!" The goal is to lure the target in with curiosity.

  • The Click:

    The victim takes the bait and clicks the link, fully expecting to see what was promised. Suspicion? None. Expectations? High.

  • The Drop:

    And there it is—Rick Astley in glorious low-res video, serenading them with "Never Gonna Give You Up." The prank has landed. Realization dawns.

  • The Aftermath:

    The victim reacts—shock, betrayal, laughter, or plotting revenge. Maybe they even pass it on. The cycle continues. The internet remains undefeated.

How Can I Rickroll Someone?

What an excellent question. There are many ways to rickroll someone, but it all begins with the most important ingredient: the link. A good rickroll needs a believable setup and a well-hidden delivery. First, choose your link carefully.

  1. Choose Your Link

    Here are a few starter links you can use to begin your rickrolling journey:

    The Classic Rickroll
    The original, the legend, the one and only. This link is iconic—so iconic, in fact, that many people can spot it a mile away. Great for beginners, but not as effective on seasoned internet users.
    R2-D2's Secret Message
    Disguised as a short Star Wars clip, this version uses a different title and thumbnail to catch fans off guard. It's clever, clean, and catches people who think they're in for galactic lore.
    Cute Little Puppy Doing Cute Things
    Who could resist clicking on a puppy video? This one takes advantage of the internet’s love of cute animals to deliver the ultimate surprise.

  2. Hide the Link

    Once you have your rickroll link, disguise it. Use a URL shortener (like bit.ly) or hyperlink it behind a believable sentence. For example: "“You’ve gotta see this lmao" or "I swear you’re gonna lose it at this"

  3. Deliver the Bait

    Now comes the fun part. Share your disguised link in a group chat, a Discord server, a class document, or even in an email. Make sure your message sounds genuine and tempting enough to click.

  4. Enjoy the Chaos

    Sit back, relax, and wait for the reactions. Some will laugh, others will groan, and a few might vow revenge. Congratulations—you’ve just continued a proud internet tradition.
